How to Buy and Sell Stocks

Learn how to navigate the STTS platform and start trading stocks in just a few steps.

What is Stock Trading?

Stock trading involves buying and selling shares of companies in the stock market. When you buy a stock, you are purchasing a small piece of ownership in a company. The goal is to sell the stock later at a higher price to make a profit. However, stock prices can fluctuate based on market conditions, company performance, and other factors.

At STTS, we make stock trading simple and accessible. As a customer, you can:

  • Buy stocks during market hours at the current market price.
  • Sell stocks to turn your investments into cash.
  • Track your portfolio to monitor your investments' performance.
Start trading today and take control of your financial future!

How to Buy Stocks
  1. Log in to your account using your credentials.
  2. Navigate to the Buy/Sell Stocks section from the dashboard.
  3. Browse the list of available stocks with their current market prices.
  4. Click the Buy button next to the stock you wish to purchase.
  5. Enter the number of shares you wish to buy.
  6. Review the transaction details, including the total cost.
  7. Confirm your purchase. The funds will be deducted from your cash account, and the shares will be added to your portfolio.
How to Sell Stocks
  1. Log in to your account using your credentials.
  2. Navigate to the Portfolio section from the dashboard.
  3. View your current stock holdings and their market values.
  4. Select the stock you wish to sell and click the Sell button.
  5. Enter the number of shares you wish to sell.
  6. Review the transaction details, including the total proceeds.
  7. Confirm the sale. The proceeds from the sale will be added to your cash account, and the sold shares will be removed from your portfolio.
Trading Tips:
  • Make sure to check the current market hours before placing trades. Orders can only be executed during active market hours.
  • Track your transaction history to monitor your trades and ensure a balanced portfolio.
